01 · Executive Summary

The thesis in one page.

Chai by Mira is a nine-year-old premium brand already stocked in Soho House, Mildreds, Foyles, and Muriel's Kitchen — 300+ cafes on a founder-led motion. The opportunity isn't more awareness; it's converting the already-earned credibility into a predictable pipeline for both wholesale and D2C. To win, Chai by Mira must own three signals: a productised wholesale funnel, Mira's founder IP as the content engine, and surgical paid spend that only fires at bottom-funnel conversion moments.

Pillar 01
Productise the wholesale motion

300 cafes won over nine years is relationship-driven. To add 150 net-new partners in 12 months, we build a dedicated /wholesale funnel, a 48-hour sample-request system, a cafe-owner Instagram strategy, and a CRM-tracked pipeline. Mira stays the closer — the system does the top-of-funnel work.

Pillar 02
Own the chai ritual conversation

Three published books, Ayurvedic credibility, Gujarati heritage, the Soho cafe legacy — no UK chai competitor has this founder IP stack. We turn Mira's voice into compounding organic assets: podcast guest rotations, recipe reels, book-driven SEO, and a dedicated /book landing page that becomes a permanent traffic magnet.

Pillar 03
Squeeze every £ of the £800/mo

At this budget, zero room for "awareness" campaigns. Every paid pound fires at a specific moment: a cafe owner visits /wholesale (retargeting), a warm visitor abandons cart (recovery), a UK searcher types "authentic chai" (Google Shopping). Organic + owned does the brand lifting for free.

The core strategic bet

The UK premium chai category is crowded at the shelf (Dishoom, Pukka, Amala Chai) but empty at the author-founder layer. Chai by Mira wins the next 12 months by behaving less like a FMCG brand and more like a publishing-backed wellness author whose product happens to be chai. Mira's book is the moat; the chai is the repeat purchase.

03 · Market & Competitive Landscape

The shelf Chai by Mira lives on.

The UK chai category is crowded at the mass-market end (Pukka, Tetley, Twinings) and at the restaurant-IP end (Dishoom). In the middle — premium, authentic, artisan-led — sits a smaller, noisier field: Amala Chai, Chai Guys, Project Chai, Jaivik. Each has one strong signal. None has the full stack Mira does. That middle shelf is where we compete, and we compete on founder IP, not on price or paid-media scale.

Strategic gap Chai by Mira owns — and must defend

Chai by Mira is the only UK chai brand that combines (a) a visibly-authentic Gujarati-heritage founder with Ayurvedic credibility, (b) a published heritage book with a major UK publisher (Headline Publishing Group, The Book of Chai), and (c) a proven 300-cafe B2B distribution base including Soho House, Mildreds, Foyles Cafe, Muriel's Kitchen, Riding House Café, and Attendant Coffee. No competitor has all three. That triangulation is the uncopyable moat. Everything downstream — content, SEO, creative, pitch decks — reinforces it.

04 · SEO Roadmap

From founder website to dominant chai shelf.

chaibymira.com is a Squarespace site with excellent brand storytelling and almost no SEO architecture. The store uses auto-generated product slugs (/p/the-ridge-vase-pbtk6), there's no /wholesale page, no /book landing page, no schema markup, and the stockist list is a JPEG-heavy page with zero local SEO equity per cafe. Huge upside — low effort.

Anti-pattern warning — avoid the siteQualityStddev trap

Do NOT duplicate one /wholesale page and swap city names. Every city page must have genuine local content: 2–3 cafe-partner mentions from that city, a local map embed, city-specific shipping/delivery copy, and ideally one photo of a local cafe using the chai. Duplicated-with-swap pages triggered Google's March 2024 Core Update site-quality penalty across thousands of SMB sites. We do it right or we don't do it.

Six content pillars, each with 6–10 hub articles feeding into commercial pages via internal linking. Every pillar ladders up to either a cafe-signup or a D2C purchase.

Pillar 01

Morning chai, afternoon chai, evening chai, how to make chai (the right way), chai preparation myths. Feeds → D2C product pages.

4.6 — Off-page & E-E-A-T

  • PR leverage through the book: pitch Mira for Stylist, Refinery29, Women's Health, The Independent, BBC Good Food, Vogue India (food / heritage / wellness angles). One piece of national press = 6 months of content marketing.
  • Podcast guest rotation: target 12 podcasts in 12 months — wellness (Feel Better Live More, Deliciously Ella, The Doctor's Kitchen), South Asian (Brown Girl Podcast, Masala Podcast), food (The Food Programme, BBC).
  • Stockist backlink reclamation: email all 300 cafe partners asking for a "we use Chai by Mira" mention + link on their site. Even 15% conversion = 45 high-trust editorial links.
  • Recipe syndication: submit recipes to BBC Good Food, Delicious Magazine, The Happy Pear, Pinterest's featured recipes — each drives referral + builds domain authority.
  • Digital PR hooks: British Chai Census (survey data), "Which UK cafe makes the best chai?" awards, National Chai Day (Sept 21) activation — built for press pickup.
  • Diaspora + wellness creator partnerships: micro-creators (5k–50k followers) with Reels-first content. Five partnerships per quarter at £100–300 each = £2k seeded into organic reach.

4.7 — Technical stack

Core Web Vitals
  • LCP under 2.5s on all landing pages (Squarespace hero images are the main culprit — compress + lazy-load)
  • CLS under 0.1 — current Squarespace theme shifts hero text after font-load; needs font preload
  • INP under 200ms — add-to-cart should fire within 200ms of click
  • Mobile-first audit every quarter — 68% of UK D2C traffic is mobile
Indexation hygiene
  • robots.txt must reference sitemap.xml explicitly
  • Squarespace default creates /config and /search URLs — noindex them
  • Cross-canonical between miramanek.com and chaibymira.com to avoid self-competition
  • Monthly Search Console coverage audit — fix Discovered / Crawled-not-indexed in 14 days

06 · Creative & Remarketing Strategy

Heritage over hustle-ad energy.

Chai by Mira's creative cannot look like a D2C brand chasing ROAS. It has to look like a publishing-grade founder brand whose product happens to be chai. That means: Mira at the centre, warm tones, slow pacing, film grain, not Canva templates. Every ad ends on the same earned signals — the book, the 300 cafes, the heritage — not on a limited-time 30% off.

6.1 — The four creative pillars

Pillar 01
Life at Chai by Mira — Lifestyle & Ritual

POV of Mira's day, her kitchen, her cafe partners, her recipe testing. Unscripted, handheld, warm-lit. Goal: self-identification by the ritualist and diaspora personas.

Pillar 02
Transformation & Outcome proof

Cafe partner spotlights. "We switched from tea bags to Chai by Mira" testimonials. Customer receipts and reviews. Converts both B2B sceptics and D2C fence-sitters.

Pillar 03
Founder IP & Heritage

Mira reading from The Book of Chai. Nani's village memory stories. Spice explainers in 60 seconds. Double-duty: organic YT Shorts + paid Meta Reels. The compounding engine.

Pillar 04
Category Education & Demand creation

"Why Pukka isn't chai." "The history of chai in 3 minutes." "What's actually in a proper masala chai." Educates the market and positions Chai by Mira as the default authority.

07 · Measurement & KPIs

If we can't measure it, we won't scale it.

Two motions, two pipelines, two definitions of "conversion". B2B success is a sample-request → first wholesale order → repeat order. D2C success is a site visit → first order → subscription. The tracking stack has to cleanly split both without double-counting, and Mira needs a single-pane Monday dashboard that tells the truth in 30 seconds.

7.1 — The tracking stack

  • Google Tag Manager — single source of truth for every event fire. No tag installs outside GTM.
  • GA4 — conversion events (wholesale_sample_request, d2c_purchase, d2c_subscribe, book_downloaded).
  • Meta Pixel + Conversions API (server-side via Shopify/Squarespace) — iOS 14+ attribution is broken client-side only.
  • Google Ads Enhanced Conversions — hash-email matching fixes 30–40% of attribution loss.
  • Klaviyo (recommended) — D2C email / SMS / owned-audience engine. Tag every first-order vs repeat.
  • HubSpot Free CRM (recommended) — B2B sample-request → wholesale-order pipeline tracking. Free tier is enough for first 12 months.
  • Call tracking — optional; only if Mira starts fielding direct wholesale calls (CallHippo or Aircall).
  • UTM discipline — every paid URL parametrised. Every cafe partner invite-email uses a UTM. No naked links.

The silent killer — wholesale response SLA

In wholesale B2B, a sample-request contacted within 2 hours is 4x more likely to convert to a first order than one contacted in 24 hours. Before scaling spend: Mira or a trade-ops person MUST commit to a 2-hour weekday SLA. HubSpot auto-responder buys the first 30 minutes; a human follow-up within the next 90 closes the loop. Without that SLA, every £28 CPL is worth 25% of what it should be — and the 150-cafe target halves.

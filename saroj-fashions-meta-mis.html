01 — Account Summary

An awareness-led account

This account runs two jobs at once. Three campaigns build brand awareness across Mumbai and Ahmedabad, reaching 18 lakh people. One remarketing campaign converts warm website visitors into sales. Each is measured on its own objective.

How to read this report

74.5% of spend (₹15,246) went to brand awareness and reach campaigns, which Meta measures on reach, ad recall, and cost per 1,000 impressions, not on sales. Only the remarketing campaign (₹5,211) is built to drive purchases, and it returned a healthy 3.28× ROAS. A single blended ROAS across all four campaigns would read 0.84× and badly understate the account, because three campaigns were never trying to sell. Revenue and ROAS in this report refer to the remarketing campaign only.

05 — Strategic Insights

Objective-aware recommendations

Six actions to get more from both the awareness spend and the remarketing engine over the next 30 days.

01 — Keep
Dark posts are excellent reach
The two dark-post campaigns reached 17.9 lakh people at ₹3.41 to ₹3.75 CPM and delivered 43,300 in estimated ad recall. This is efficient, high-quality brand awareness. Keep both running and treat them as the reach backbone of the account.
→ Maintain budgets, rotate creative monthly
02 — Fix
Awareness Ahmedabad CPM is 16× too high
The standard Awareness Campaign in Ahmedabad pays ₹57.74 per 1,000 impressions versus ₹3.41 to ₹3.75 for the dark-post format. On its ₹5,087 spend, the dark-post format would reach over a million more people. Either diagnose the setup or move this budget into the dark-post format.
→ Audit targeting/placement or reallocate
03 — Bridge
Connect awareness to remarketing
18 lakh people see the brand, but only 4,971 entered the remarketing pool, which is why purchases are capped at 9. Add a mid-funnel traffic or engagement campaign so awareness viewers visit saroj.in and become retargetable. This is the single highest-leverage change in the account.
→ Launch a ₹150–₹200/day traffic campaign
04 — Scale
Remarketing is profitable, just small
3.28× ROAS on ₹5,211 with ₹17,107 revenue is a healthy return for a fashion retargeting campaign. The constraint is audience size, not creative. As the mid-funnel grows the warm pool, this campaign can absorb more budget at a similar ROAS.
→ Hold budget now, scale as the pool grows
05 — Watch
Remarketing CPP and AOV
Cost per purchase is ₹579 against a ₹1,901 average order value, so each sale is still comfortably profitable, but the margin is thinner than the headline ROAS suggests once product cost is added. Track CPP as the audience scales and protect the ₹1,901 AOV with bundle or minimum-cart offers.
→ Test a free-shipping threshold to lift AOV
06 — Capture
Follow up the 39 messaging contacts
The campaigns produced 39 inbound messaging contacts as a by-product of reach. These are warm inquiries that cost nothing extra. A simple WhatsApp or Messenger follow-up flow could convert a share of them into orders that never show up in the ROAS figure.
→ Set up a response SLA and a saved reply flow
Overall assessment

This is a healthy brand-awareness account with a profitable remarketing tail. The dark-post format is delivering 18 lakh in reach at a ₹3 to ₹4 CPM, and the remarketing campaign returns 3.28× on a small warm audience. The two clearest wins are reallocating the costly Ahmedabad awareness campaign into the efficient dark-post format, and building a mid-funnel bridge so the large awareness reach feeds the remarketing pool. Do both and the same budget should produce meaningfully more purchases next period.
